About the course

There is one key goal associated with this course. Build a data driven project.

  • Go to the drawing board. Conceive an idea that solves a problem.
  • Mine, simulate, create, scrape, (or use existing) data that can help you solve this problem.
  • Solve it- leverage your skills from all your learnings (ML, DL, Statistics, Cybersecurity, Data Mining, Programming and DBMS, DMQL and so on) to infer results from data and elaborate on your findings.

This course aims to develop both technical and soft skills that are not directly taught in traditional courses but are relevant to modern data science. The intent of the course is to create an ecosystem where skills learnt through prior courses are tied together to build functional data driven projects.¬†

Course Objectives¬†

Through the course, students work to:

  • complete a research experience in a course-like setting with a primary data science focus. The experience should build on concepts and topics taught during previous coursework, such as data analytics and mining, machine learning, data-driven, data-enabled, or data-centric work, scientific computing, among others.
  • demonstrate a working knowledge of relevant tools, techniques, and literature in the field of data science
  • develop an understanding of discipline-specific methodology and analyses as part of a concrete application project.
  • design and execute a fluid analytic project plan and adapt this plan to the project throughout the duration of the project. This may include the need for additional independent learning of new techniques and analytical methods required for cutting edge analysis.
  • interpret results and outcomes in the context of the domain of the application.
  • regularly disseminate results and challenges with supervisors and colleagues.
