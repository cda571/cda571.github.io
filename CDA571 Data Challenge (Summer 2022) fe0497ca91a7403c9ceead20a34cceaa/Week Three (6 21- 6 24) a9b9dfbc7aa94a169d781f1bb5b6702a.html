Week Three (6/21- 6/24)

This week is all about teams and projects. By the time you see this page, you might have completed the project proposal phase- kudos!

This week’s objectives:

  • Finalize the problem and dataset.
  • Provide some background and introduction to the problem.
  • What challenge does this address in the field?
  • Identify any analytical gaps – this could be “in the field” or in your own background that you would need to tackle the problem.

This week’s deliverables:

  • Project Proposal
    • Articulate the problem in terms of the data that you have identified, and the variables.
    • Get into detail wrt the data – size, type of variables etc.. Potentially identify a “backup” dataset.

  • Schedule your 1:1 meeting- Each team will be assigned one instructor who will be your point person for the course. Your instructor will reach out to you with instructions on how to schedule them.

1:1 Meetings

  • What is this?
    • Your instructor will meet with your whole team, and assess your progress, and provide feedback on your work thus far. This feedback is to help keep the project in line with the course’s objectives.

  • Does everyone in the team have to make it?
    • Yes.

  • How do I schedule one?
    • Your instructor will reach out to you with further instructions.

  • What should I do in the meeting?
    • In each 1:1 meeting, be prepared to discuss:
      • Project Progress - How much have you completed, and what you intend to do next before the next 1:1.
      • Roles and Responsibilities: Inform your instructors of your task distribution plan- which member is doing what.
      • Concerns - Anything that is a hinderance to the project.
      • Anything else relevant to the project, is always welcome.

  • What does the instructor do?
    • The instructor processes your plan, and rubber stamps it after providing any feedback on any subsequent task in the project. Integrating this feedback is critical to the course objectives, and grading schemes.

  • Anything else?
    • Nope.
