Week Four (6/27- 7/1)

This week is all about work, progress, and WIPs. Project proposals are done, and feedbacks have been delivered. The next step is to start analyses, and present preliminary results.

This week’s objectives:

  • Finalize the problem and dataset.
  • Provide some background and introduction to the problem.
  • What challenge does this address in the field?
  • Identify any analytical gaps – this could be “in the field” or in your own background that you would need to tackle the problem.
  • Begin EDA, and readying your data for analysis.
  • What analysis do you plan to do?

This week’s deliverables:

  • If your instructor has delivered feedback, do make sure that you are working on it and get their approval.
  • Start preliminary analysis, and prepare your WIP 1 Talk.

WIP 1

  • Go through the video on what a WIP presentation entails. The link to this is on the Week 1 page.
  • Please follow the rubric below to prepare your presentation. The areas mentioned below have to be covered- you can add anything more to create more context.

The presentation should mimic the structure of a scientific paper- with an Introduction. Please do go trough the guide below if you are new to the process.

Writing a scientific article: A step-by-step guide for beginners

The following areas are mandatory to cover - anything else can be added to this.

  • Problem Definition
  • Problem Motivation
  • Data/Dataset Description
  • EDA Results
  • Future Analysis plans
    • Next preprocessing steps
    • Model Choices (detail which method(s) you intend to take, and justify it)
  • Expected output
  • Current progress of every member in the team
  • Roles and Responsibilities for every member of the team
  • Challenges/Future Directions
  • Timeline of plans for the next four weeks
